a little backstory -

it's incredible how many things i've tried. checklists that turned into paragraphs, notebooks i lost, apps i abandoned after three days, a notion loop setup i spent a weekend on and opened exactly twice. i kept making the same lists in different places and forgetting i'd made them. nothing stuck - and i couldn't figure out if that was the apps or just me.


turns out, i didn't need another system to help me. i just needed somewhere to put things down after they'd already happened - on the days i managed a hundred things and on the days i managed one. coz both count. both deserved to go somewhere without the guilt of everything i didn't get to sitting right next to it. a done list, not a to-do list. you open it after - after the shower you finally managed to have, the email you sent after two months, the three-hour walk, the plants you actually remembered to water this time. the mundane stuff that deserves to exist somewhere, acknowledged, without needing to be part of a streak or a score or a habit that gets tracked and judged.


that's afterward. your data stays on your phone, not on someone else's server doing something you didn't sign up for. it's supposed to feel like a dotted journal - think muji, but in your pocket - because books don't require internet to function. straightforward, clean, calm, quiet in earth colours, coz what else feels more grounding than the earth greens? oh and did i mention customisable? fonts, dot colours, tones - enough to feel like yours; not so much you need to decide. we even have a font for the dyslexics so nobody feels left out - because the tool should feel like yours, not the other way around. there's even a place to just unload your head and rant (like i just did), if you need it.


some of our brains don't work the way productivity apps assume they do - and that's not a personal failing. afterward doesn't fix that; it doesn't even try to. it just doesn't make it worse, and definitely doesn't try to turn you into who you aren't.
